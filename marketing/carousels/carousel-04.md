## Slide 2
**Headline:** They talk less.
**Body copy:** The top 1% of closers maintain a talk-to-listen ratio of 43:57. That means they listen more than they speak. The average rep talks 65–75% of the call. More talking correlates directly with lower close rates.

*(Source: Gong.io Revenue Intelligence research)*

**Visual direction:**
- Background: #0a0a0a
- Split bar visual: "Average rep" left (65% filled in #444444) vs "Top closer" right (43% filled in #814ac8) — labelled clearly
- "Talk time" metric visible under each bar
- Body copy at 16px in #c4b8e0
- Callout in #df7afe: "The less you talk, the more they buy."
- "Stat 1 of 5" pill label, top-right

## Slide 3
**Headline:** They use the prospect's name — but not constantly.
**Body copy:** Top closers use the prospect's first name an average of 4 times per call. Reps who never use the name lose connection. Reps who overuse it come across as scripted. Four times is the sweet spot — opening, a critical moment, an objection, and the close.

*(Source: Sales psychology research / Gong data)*

**Visual direction:**
- Background: #111111
- Large "4×" in gradient at 100px
- Timeline visual: a horizontal call arc with 4 marked points — "Opener," "Insight moment," "Objection," "Close" — each labelled with the prospect's name in a small tag
- Body copy at 16px in #c4b8e0
- "Stat 2 of 5" pill

## Slide 4
**Headline:** They ask more questions.
**Body copy:** Top performers ask an average of 11–14 questions per discovery call. Average reps ask 6–8. More questions means more information, more rapport, and a clearer picture of the real buying motivation. Every unanswered question is a reason to say no.

*(Source: Gong.io analysis of 25,000+ sales calls)*

**Visual direction:**
- Background: #0a0a0a
- Large "11–14" in gradient at 90px
- Simple comparison: two question-mark bubble icons — small (6–8, labelled "Average") vs large (11–14, labelled "Top 1%")
- Body copy at 16px in #c4b8e0
- "Stat 3 of 5" pill

---

## Slide 5
**Headline:** They respond to objections faster.
**Body copy:** Top closers pause 1.1 seconds before responding to an objection. Average reps jump in at 0.5 seconds or interrupt entirely. The pause signals confidence, not uncertainty — it communicates that you heard them and you're not afraid of what they said.

*(Source: Gong.io conversational intelligence data)*

**Visual direction:**
- Background: #111111
- Stopwatch visual: "0.5s" with a red border (average rep) vs "1.1s" with a green border (top closer)
- Below: "Pausing = confidence. Rushing = insecurity." in #df7afe bold
- Body copy at 16px
- "Stat 4 of 5" pill

## Slide 6
**Headline:** Their win rate correlates with call speed.
**Body copy:** Top closers speak at 170–180 words per minute — slightly slower than conversational average. Reps who speak above 200 WPM are perceived as nervous or pushy. Slowing down even slightly shifts how the prospect perceives your confidence and control.

*(Source: Gong.io speech analytics)*

**Visual direction:**
- Background: #0a0a0a
- Speed dial graphic: a simple arc from Slow to Fast with a pointer landing in the "sweet spot" zone (170–180 WPM) highlighted in #814ac8
- "Too fast" zone in #ff4444, "Too slow" zone in #444444, "Sweet spot" in #39d353
- Body copy at 16px in #c4b8e0
- "Stat 5 of 5" pill
