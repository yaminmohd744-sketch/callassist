## Slide 2
**Headline:** The first 3 seconds: don't explain who you are.
**Body copy:** Most reps open with: "Hi, this is [Name] from [Company] — do you have a few minutes?" This gives the prospect nothing to care about before asking them to commit. Lead with relevance, not identity. Identity follows if they want to know.

## Slide 3
**Headline:** Lead with a pattern interrupt.
**Body copy:** Instead of "Hi this is John from Acme" — try: "Hey [Name], you're probably going to want to hang up in about 20 seconds, but give me that long." It sounds confident. It sounds human. And it triggers curiosity instead of the auto-defensive reflex.

## Slide 4
**Headline:** Seconds 4–10: give them a reason to stay on.
**Body copy:** "I work with [role type similar to theirs] at [recognisable company type] who are dealing with [specific problem]. If that's not relevant, I'll let you go — but if it is, it's worth 5 minutes." You've done three things: shown you know them, named a real problem, and made it low-stakes to say yes.

## Slide 5
**Headline:** Seconds 11–20: earn curiosity, not commitment.
**Body copy:** You're not trying to pitch the product here. You're trying to get them to agree the problem is real. "Is [specific problem] something you're actively dealing with, or has it not been a priority yet?" One question. Designed to open a door, not close a deal.

## Slide 6
**Headline:** Seconds 21–30: set the agenda or earn the objection.
**Body copy:** If they're still on the line, they're curious. Now give them a framing: "Takes about 15 minutes — I'll ask a few questions, share what I'm seeing with similar teams, and if it's not a fit I'll tell you." This is confident, respectful, and removes pressure. The agenda is the close.

## Slide 7
**Headline:** The 4 words that kill every cold call opening.
**Body copy:** "Is now a bad time?" It's a yes/no question where "yes" ends the call, "no" means you've asked permission rather than earned it, and the whole framing tells them this might not be worth their time. Never ask this. Assume the call is worth their time. The energy comes through.
