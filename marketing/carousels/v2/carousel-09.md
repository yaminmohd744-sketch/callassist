## Slide 3
**Headline:** Budget signals — what to listen for.
**Body copy:** Direct: "We have budget approved." Indirect: "We had a similar tool last year but the cost came up during renewal." Buried: "Our CFO is very focused on ROI this quarter." The indirect and buried versions are more common and more honest. Most reps miss them because they're focused on their next question.

## Slide 4
**Headline:** Timeline signals — what to listen for.
**Body copy:** "We need to have something in place before Q3." — active timeline. "We've been looking at this for a while." — delayed pain, low urgency. "I have a board review in 6 weeks." — hidden deadline. When you hear timeline language, you've found the hook for every follow-up conversation.
**Visual direction:**
- Background: #0a0a0a

- "You've found the hook for every follow-up." — bolded, #df7afe

## Slide 5
**Headline:** Authority signals — who actually decides.
**Body copy:** "I'll need to loop in my manager." = you're not talking to the decision-maker. "My team would need to be on board." = they have influence but not authority. "I can move on this." = you might have a champion AND a closer in one. Map the org before you pitch.
**Visual direction:**
- Background: #111111
- Top label: "SIGNAL TYPE 3: AUTHORITY" — small caps, #814ac8
- Headline: #f0ebff ExtraBold, 34px
- Three quotes each with a translation label — same callout treatment as Slide 4
- "Map the org before you pitch." — isolated line, #df7afe, directive

## Slide 6
**Headline:** Pain intensity signals — how much does it actually hurt?
**Body copy:** "It's a bit of an issue." = low priority, won't move fast. "It's costing us time every week." = moderate pain, budget available. "We had to bring in a consultant last month." = high pain, already spending, ready to buy. The intensity of the pain predicts the speed of the sale.
**Visual direction:**
- Background: #0a0a0a
- Top label: "SIGNAL TYPE 4: PAIN INTENSITY" — small caps, #814ac8
- Headline: #f0ebff ExtraBold, 32px
- Three-level pain scale visual: three horizontal bars, low/medium/high — filled in #814ac8 (low), #df7afe (medium), white (high) — like a signal strength indicator
- Quote examples beside each level
- "The intensity of the pain predicts the speed of the sale." — bolded, payoff line

## Slide 7
**Headline:** Competitor signals — what they reveal about the real decision.
**Body copy:** "We're also looking at [Competitor]." = there's a live evaluation and you're one of many. "We tried [Competitor] before." = there's a previous failure shaping their requirements. "Our current provider is [Competitor]." = switching cost is on their mind before you've said a word. These change your entire approach.
**Visual direction:**

## Slide 8
**Headline:** The easiest way to get better at listening.
**Body copy:** After every call, write down three things the prospect said that you didn't fully explore. Not the objections — the off-hand comments. The "well, ideally..." statements. The "we've been meaning to..." clauses. These are the threads you pull in the follow-up. That's where the deal actually lives.
**Visual direction:**
