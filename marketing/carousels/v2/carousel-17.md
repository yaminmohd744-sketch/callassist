## Slide 2
**Headline:** Mistake 1: Your lighting makes you look untrustworthy.
**Body copy:** Dark, backlit, or inconsistent lighting doesn't just look bad — it unconsciously signals low investment and low credibility. Studies on video persuasion show that poorly lit faces score 23% lower on "trustworthy" and "prepared" ratings. The fix is a $30 ring light. It's the highest-ROI purchase in your sales kit.

## Slide 3
**Headline:** Mistake 2: You're looking at the screen, not the camera.
**Body copy:** Eye contact on video means looking into the camera, not at the person's face on your screen. When you look at their face, your eyes appear to be slightly down and off-centre to them — the universal signal of evasiveness. Stick a small dot sticker beside your camera lens. That's where to look during the close.
**Visual direction:**

## Slide 4
**Headline:** Mistake 3: Slouching signals low energy and low confidence.
**Body copy:** Posture isn't about formality — it's about the energy your body occupies in the frame. Slouching compresses your frame, muffles your voice slightly, and unconsciously reads as "I'm not fully here." Sit forward slightly, shoulders back, camera at eye level. You'll sound different within 30 seconds.

## Slide 5
**Headline:** Mistake 4: Talking too fast reads as anxiety.
**Body copy:** Speech rate under stress increases by an average of 25%. Prospects can hear it even when they can't name it. Fast speech signals nerves, not excitement. Slow down by 20% from your natural pace on calls. Pause after they finish speaking — one full second. That pause reads as confidence. In every culture. Without exception.

## Slide 6
**Headline:** Mistake 5: Nodding constantly says you're desperate for approval.
**Body copy:** Constant nodding is a submission signal. It says "I agree with everything, please keep liking me." In a sales context, it undermines your authority and makes every "yes" you give meaningless. Reserve your nod for genuinely interesting points. Let silence do the work when they say something you already know.
**Visual direction:**

## Slide 7
**Headline:** The two-minute video call checklist.
**Body copy:** Before every call: Light is in front of you (not behind). Camera is at eye level. Shoulders are back and open. Pasted a dot sticker near your camera lens. Audio is clear (headphones preferred over laptop mic). Background is clean or blurred. These six things take 2 minutes and change how every call starts.
