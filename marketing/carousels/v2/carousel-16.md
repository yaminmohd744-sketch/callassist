# Carousel 16: Sales vs. Normal Jobs

**Category:** Humour / Relatable
**Platform:** Instagram + TikTok
**Target persona:** Sales people — broad, all levels. Also extremely shareable to non-sales audiences who have sales friends.
**Hook goal:** Humour / Validation

---

## Hook (Slide 1)
**Headline:** Sales vs. normal jobs.
**Sub-text:** Nobody prepared me for this.

**Visual direction:**
- Background: #000000
- Headline: #f0ebff Figtree ExtraBold, 60px, centred — large, confident, simple
- "normal jobs" in #df7afe — slightly sarcastic emphasis
- Sub-text: white Regular italic, 22px — delivered deadpan
- Visual element: two columns forming in the background — left: a faint office icon, right: a faint phone/dial icon — at 8% opacity, establishing the comparison format
- Motion note: the word "Sales" slides in from the left, "normal jobs" from the right. They collide in the centre.

---

## Slide 2
**Headline:** Monday morning.
**Body copy:** Normal job: fine. Your Monday is a Monday. It's fine. Nothing happened over the weekend that affects your Tuesday. Sales: your Monday depends entirely on whether you closed something on Friday. And whether that deal held over the weekend. And whether your pipeline is on fire or on ice. Same calendar, completely different reality.
**Visual direction:**
- Background: #0a0a0a
- Two-column layout:
  - Left column header: "NORMAL JOB" in #39d353 small-caps
  - Right column header: "SALES" in #df7afe small-caps
  - Left body: "Your Monday is fine." in white Regular
  - Right body: "Depends on Friday's close rate and whether your champion is still employed at the prospect company." in white Regular
- Visual divider line in #814ac8 between columns
- Tone is completely deadpan — the humour is in the specificity

---

## Slide 3
**Headline:** Payday.
**Body copy:** Normal job: your salary arrives as agreed. On a predictable date. For a predictable amount. You can plan things. Sales: your take-home is somewhere between "great, I'm taking the family on holiday" and "I'm stress-eating crackers and reviewing my savings account at 11pm."
**Visual direction:**
- Background: #111111
- Same two-column format
- Left: "Predictable. Stable. Boring." — white Regular
- Right: "A mystery even to you until the commission statement arrives." — white Regular
- The right column should have a slight extra energy — longer text, more drama
- "Stress-eating crackers" is the line that earns the share — make sure it's readable

---

## Slide 4
**Headline:** Your manager's favourite phrase.
**Body copy:** Normal job: "Great work on that project, the team appreciates it." Sales: "How's your pipeline looking?" Not hello. Not good morning. Pipeline. It's the first thing they ask when you walk in, the last thing they ask before a weekend, and the thing they're definitely thinking during your birthday lunch.
**Visual direction:**
- Background: #0a0a0a
- Two-column format
- Left: "Great work on that project." — clean
- Right: "How's your pipeline?" — slightly larger text, slight #df7afe tint — this is the punchline
- "Definitely thinking during your birthday lunch" — italicised, smaller, white — the tag that lands the joke

---

## Slide 5
**Headline:** Lunch break.
**Body copy:** Normal job: you eat lunch. At lunch. Sales: you eat lunch while updating your CRM, scheduling follow-ups, and wondering if you should call that prospect back now or wait until 2pm when they said they'd be free, which they definitely won't be, so you call at 2pm anyway and get voicemail.
**Visual direction:**
- Background: #111111
- Two-column format
- Left: "Lunch." — just the one word. Maximum comedy in minimum text.
- Right: the full run-on description — matching the chaotic energy of the situation
- The run-on format on the right is deliberate — matches the spiral of the experience

---

## Slide 6
**Headline:** End of quarter.
**Body copy:** Normal job: you're aware it's the end of a quarter, vaguely. Sales: you've lost 3kg because you stopped eating. You've memorised every open opportunity by name, probability, and exact close date. You haven't slept properly in 11 days. You know exactly what you need to close and in what order. You're exhausted, wired, and somehow this is your favourite part.
**Visual direction:**
- Background: #0a0a0a
- Two-column format
- Left: "Oh, is it end of quarter?" — faint, unbothered
- Right: "I have a spreadsheet. It has colours." — this is the line. Simple. True.
- Body detail below the right column: the exhausted-but-alive description in smaller text
- "Somehow this is your favourite part." — isolated italic, #df7afe

---

## Slide 7
**Headline:** Asking for a raise.
**Body copy:** Normal job: book a meeting with HR. Present your case. Wait 6–8 weeks. Receive a 3% cost-of-living adjustment. Sales: close more deals. The conversation happened automatically at 120% of quota. The math does the negotiating for you.
**Visual direction:**
- Background: #111111
- Two-column format
- Left: "6–8 weeks, 3%, awkward conversation, unchanged." — white Regular
- Right: "Hit quota. Math negotiates for you." — #df7afe, slightly larger — this is actually the aspirational slide hiding in the comedy format
- This slide tips the tone — sales actually wins this comparison

---

## Slide 8
**Headline:** The thing about sales people.
**Body copy:** We chose the hardest version of working for a living. The income is unpredictable. The emotional labour is real. The Mondays depend on Fridays. But the ceiling is removed. And the people who last aren't the ones who found it easy — they're the ones who found the chaos worth it.
**Visual direction:**
- Background: #0a0a0a
- Tone shifts — no columns. Full-width text.
- Headline: #f0ebff ExtraBold, 38px
- "The ceiling is removed." — isolated, large, #df7afe — the payoff that reframes everything
- Body copy: white Regular
- This slide is the emotional resonance that earns the share from the laugh of the previous slides

Or that "pipeline" would become the most-used word in my vocabulary. Or that end of quarter would somehow be my favourite and least favourite time of year simultaneously.

If you've worked in sales, you get it. Tag someone who also gets it.

## TikTok Adaptation Notes
- Extremely high share and tag rate — this format is inherently social
- Creator reacts to each comparison — the comedy is in the recognition, not in the punchline being delivered
- "Two truths" splitscreen format works perfectly for TikTok
- Voiceover tone: dry, slightly defeated but loving it — the voice of someone who has genuinely lived this
- Music vibe: something slightly slapstick — office comedy soundtrack energy
- Monday comparison and end-of-quarter slides are the two strongest standalone clips
- Comments will be full of additions: "what about the prospect who ghosts after 8 touches" — engage with all of them
