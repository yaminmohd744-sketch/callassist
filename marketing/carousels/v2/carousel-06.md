# Carousel 06: Green Flags vs Red Flags in a Sales Job

**Category:** Career
**Platform:** Instagram + TikTok
**Target persona:** SDRs and AEs considering job changes, people evaluating offers, early-career sales reps — also HR/recruiters will share this defensively (which creates reach)
**Hook goal:** Validation / Curiosity

## Slide 2 — Red Flag
**Headline:** "We have an amazing culture here."
**Body copy:** Translation: The base pay is below market and we're hoping vibes compensate. Always ask: what's the base? What's the realistic OTE based on actual rep performance — not top performers? Culture doesn't pay rent.
**Visual direction:**
- Background: #0a0a0a
- Top-left corner: "RED FLAG" label in #ff4444, small-caps, bold
- Headline: #f0ebff ExtraBold, 36px, quote style
- Body copy: white Regular
- "Culture doesn't pay rent." — bolded, isolated line, #df7afe
- Faint #ff4444 tint in the left border or edge glow — very subtle signal of the flag type

---

## Slide 3 — Red Flag
**Headline:** "Uncapped commission!"
**Body copy:** On a $20 average order value. Or with an 8-month sales cycle and a 3% close rate. Uncapped commission is only meaningful if the mechanics underneath it are real. Ask: what did the median rep make in commission last year?
**Visual direction:**
- Background: #111111
- Top-left: "RED FLAG" label in #ff4444
- Headline: #f0ebff ExtraBold, 36px, exclamation mark should feel ironic
- Body copy: white Regular
- "What did the median rep make in commission last year?" — bolded, #df7afe, this is the question to actually ask
- Same faint red edge glow treatment

---

## Slide 4 — Red Flag
**Headline:** "We'll train you — don't worry."
**Body copy:** Translation: we have a 3-day Zoom onboarding, a shared Google Drive, and a manager who expects you to figure it out. Ask: is there a structured ramp plan? A defined 30/60/90? Do you shadow calls before you run them? If not — that's on you to build from scratch.
**Visual direction:**
- Background: #0a0a0a
- Top-left: "RED FLAG" label in #ff4444
- Headline: #f0ebff ExtraBold, 36px, quoted
- "That's on you to build from scratch." — isolated, #df7afe, the gut-punch line
- Body copy: white Regular

---

## Slide 5 — Red Flag
**Headline:** The hiring manager can't tell you who their best rep is.
**Body copy:** If they can't immediately name a top performer and explain what makes them effective — there's no coaching culture. Good sales organisations celebrate their best reps openly and are specific about why they win. Vagueness about performance is a signal.
**Visual direction:**
- Background: #111111
- Top-left: "RED FLAG" label in #ff4444
- Headline: #f0ebff ExtraBold, 34px (slightly smaller for longer headline)
- "Vagueness about performance is a signal." — bolded, #df7afe
- Body copy: white Regular

---

## Slide 6 — Green Flag
**Headline:** They can describe the ICP in two sentences.
**Body copy:** "We sell to VP of Operations at mid-market logistics companies, 200–1,000 employees, dealing with routing inefficiency." If they can say that — they know who they're selling to. That means your pipeline won't be full of accidental meetings.
**Visual direction:**
- Background: #0a0a0a
- Top-left: "GREEN FLAG" label in #39d353 — brighter, warmer energy
- Headline: #f0ebff ExtraBold, 34px
- The example ICP description in quote marks, #df7afe — shows what "good" looks like concretely
- Body copy: white Regular
- Faint #39d353 left border glow instead of the red from previous slides

---

## Slide 7 — Green Flag
**Headline:** The manager was a top rep — and still coaches actively.
**Body copy:** The best sales managers were excellent individual contributors who chose to grow others. If your manager can jump on a call with you and demonstrate — not just advise — you'll learn faster than any training programme.
**Visual direction:**
- Background: #111111
- Top-left: "GREEN FLAG" label in #39d353
- Headline: #f0ebff ExtraBold, 34px
- "Demonstrate — not just advise." — bolded, #df7afe, the key distinction
- Body copy: white Regular

---

## Slide 8 — Green Flag
**Headline:** There's a real tech stack and a CRM everyone actually uses.
**Body copy:** Salesforce with actual data in it. A dialer. Some sort of sequencing tool. If reps are logging calls in spreadsheets and "we're implementing Salesforce soon" — the ops infrastructure doesn't exist, which means no data, no coaching, and no accountability.
**Visual direction:**
- Background: #0a0a0a
- Top-left: "GREEN FLAG" label in #39d353
- Headline: #f0ebff ExtraBold, 32px
- "'We're implementing Salesforce soon'" — in quotes, #df7afe, italic — the red-flag-inside-a-green-flag-slide moment, slightly ironic
- Body copy: white Regular

---

## Slide 9
**Headline:** The questions to ask in every sales interview.
**Body copy:** 1. What did the median rep make in commission last year? 2. What's the average ramp time? 3. Can I speak to a current rep — not one chosen by the recruiter? 4. What does the first 90 days look like in writing? 5. What percentage of reps hit quota last year?
**Visual direction:**
- Background: #111111
- Headline: #f0ebff ExtraBold, 34px
- Numbered list format — large #814ac8 numbers, white text
- Each question on its own line with clear spacing
- This is a high-save slide — design it to feel like a checklist people screenshot
