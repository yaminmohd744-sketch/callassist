## Slide 3
**Headline:** Reps forget 70% of training within 24 hours.
**Body copy:** This is the Ebbinghaus Forgetting Curve — documented research from 1885 that the sales training industry has known about for decades and largely ignored. By the end of a training week, reps retain about 10% of what they were taught. The training isn't failing because reps are lazy. It's failing because information without application is not learning.
**Visual direction:**
- Background: #111111
- "70%" in Fragment Mono, massive, #df7afe — the stat is the visual
- The Forgetting Curve as a simple line — starts high, drops steeply over the first 24 hours, flattens — in #814ac8 line art below the headline
- Labelled points: "Training day", "24 hours", "1 week", "1 month" — each on the curve
- "Information without application is not learning." — bolded, #f0ebff, isolated — the takeaway
- Headline: #f0ebff ExtraBold, 34px
- Body copy: white Regular

## Slide 7
**Headline:** The cost of a missed close, compounded.
**Body copy:** If your average deal is $5,000 and you miss 3 closes a month due to poor objection handling — that's $15,000/month. $180,000/year. From three avoidable losses every 30 days. The rep who gets 2 of those 3 back through better in-call handling makes $120,000 more in revenue than the one who doesn't. That's not a quota difference. That's a training gap.

## TikTok Adaptation Notes
**Format:** Stat-reveal — each number appears large, creator reacts or provides context voiceover
**Voiceover tone:** Serious but not alarmist — like a data analyst who also happens to sell
**Clip structure:** One stat per cut — tight, fast, each number is the hook for 2 seconds before context fills in
**Text overlays:** Big number appears first in full screen, then body copy slides up below it
**Music vibe:** Slightly tense, ambient — something that feels like information incoming
**Hook frame:** "Five sales statistics that should make every sales manager pause" — or just the "$115,000" number on screen with no context for 2 seconds before the explanation
**Standalone clips:** Every slide is a standalone TikTok — each stat works independently as a 15-second clip
**End card:** "Find the stat that matters most — the ones hiding in your calls. pitchr.org"
