## Slide 2
**Headline:** Why most reps stop at 2.
**Body copy:** It feels rude to follow up a third time. It feels desperate at four. Nobody trained you on what to say on touch 6. And when the prospect goes quiet, the silence reads like rejection — so you protect yourself by giving up first. Meanwhile, your prospect is just busy.
**Visual direction:**
- Background: #0a0a0a
- Headline: #df7afe ExtraBold, 36px

- Body copy: white Regular

## Slide 3
**Headline:** The first follow-up (same day): confirm and add value.
**Body copy:** Don't just send "great speaking to you." Send: a recap of what they said mattered most, a specific resource relevant to that problem, and a clear ask. Time it for 2 hours post-call. You're still in their memory. Move fast.
**Visual direction:**
- Background: #111111
- Top label: "TOUCH 1 — DAY 0" — #814ac8 small-caps, bold
- Headline: #f0ebff ExtraBold, 34px
- Three-point checklist:
  - Recap their words (not yours)
  - One relevant resource
  - One clear ask
- Each item has a #814ac8 checkbox marker
- "Move fast." — bolded, #df7afe, isolated

---

## Slide 4
**Headline:** Touch 2 (Day 3): a new data point, not a nudge.
**Body copy:** "Just checking in" is a nudge. It says: I have nothing new for you but please respond. Instead: share something new. A case study from their industry. An insight from a similar conversation. A stat they'd care about. Make them glad they opened it.
**Visual direction:**
- Background: #0a0a0a
- Top label: "TOUCH 2 — DAY 3" — #814ac8 small-caps
- Headline: #f0ebff ExtraBold, 34px
- "Just checking in" — struck through in #ff4444 tint — visual "no"
- "A data point, not a nudge." — #df7afe bold — the replacement principle
- Body copy: white Regular

## Slide 5
**Headline:** Touch 3 (Day 7): challenge their status quo.
**Body copy:** By touch 3, they've either forgotten you or are avoiding you. Either way — pattern interrupt. Ask something they haven't considered: "If [problem] stays unresolved for the next 90 days, what does that cost you?" It restarts the conversation by restarting the pain.
**Visual direction:**
- Background: #111111
- Top label: "TOUCH 3 — DAY 7" — #814ac8 small-caps
- Headline: #f0ebff ExtraBold, 34px
- The challenge question in a callout box — #814ac8 border, #df7afe text
- "It restarts the conversation by restarting the pain." — bolded, payoff line
- Body copy: white Regular

## Slide 6
**Headline:** Touch 4–6 (Days 10–21): vary the channel.
**Body copy:** Email only is lazy. Switch to: LinkedIn connection request with a personalised note. A voicemail at 8am (they rarely block voicemail). A short video email — 90 seconds, looking into camera. A physical note if the deal is large enough. Channel variety isn't spam — it's persistence with respect.
**Visual direction:**
- Background: #0a0a0a
- Top label: "TOUCHES 4–6 — DAYS 10–21" — #814ac8 small-caps
- Headline: #f0ebff ExtraBold, 32px
- Channel list with icons in a 2x2 grid:
  - LinkedIn | Voicemail
  - Video email | Physical note
- Each channel has a brief note beside it in white Regular
- "Persistence with respect." — isolated, #df7afe, bold

## Slide 7
**Headline:** The "break-up" email — touch 8+.
**Body copy:** "I've reached out a few times without hearing back. I don't want to clutter your inbox if timing isn't right — so this is my last note unless you want to reconnect. No pressure, no hard feelings. If things change: [your email]." This email has a higher response rate than touches 3–7 combined.
**Visual direction:**
- Background: #111111
- Headline: #df7afe ExtraBold, 34px

- "Higher response rate than touches 3–7 combined." — below the box, bolded, #f0ebff — the proof point
- Body copy: white Regular beneath
