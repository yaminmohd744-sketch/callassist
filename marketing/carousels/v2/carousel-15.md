## Slide 2
**Headline:** 0:00 – 0:30 | The Opening
**Body copy:** The rep didn't apologise for calling. Didn't ask if it was a good time. Said: "Hey [Name] — I know this is cold, but I've got a specific reason for reaching out. Give me 30 seconds and I'll tell you why." Clear. Confident. Specific. The prospect said: "Go ahead." The call started from a position of mutual respect, not begged permission.

## Slide 3
**Headline:** 0:30 – 2:00 | Permission + Agenda
**Body copy:** "I work with [role] at companies like yours dealing with [problem]. If it's relevant, I'd like to ask a few questions — takes about 10 minutes, and I'll tell you honestly if I think we can help. Sound fair?" Two things happened: they gave a reason to stay on, and they made it feel collaborative instead of adversarial. The prospect said yes.

## Slide 4
**Headline:** 2:00 – 5:00 | Discovery
**Body copy:** Three questions. No more. "What does your current process look like?" "Where does it break down?" "What have you tried so far?" Each question was followed by silence — real silence. The rep let the prospect finish every sentence. They took notes openly ("that's interesting — let me write that down"). By minute 5, the prospect had sold themselves on the problem.
**Visual direction:**
- Background: #0a0a0a

- "The prospect had sold themselves on the problem." — isolated, #df7afe, bold — the insight

## Slide 5
**Headline:** 5:00 – 7:00 | Presentation
**Body copy:** Two minutes. Not a feature dump — a mirror. "Based on what you told me, the two things that sound most relevant are [X] and [Y]. Let me show you those specifically." They didn't demo the whole product. They demoed the two things the prospect had described as their exact pain points. The prospect nodded (you could hear it). "That's exactly what we need."
**Visual direction:**

- Headline: #f0ebff ExtraBold, 34px
- Mirror technique template in callout box: "Based on what you told me..." format
- "'That's exactly what we need.'" — in quote marks, large, #df7afe — the moment the deal turned

## Slide 6
**Headline:** 7:00 – 9:00 | Objection Handling
**Body copy:** "The price is a bit higher than we expected." The rep didn't argue. Said: "Totally fair — let me ask you something. Over the last year, what has [the problem they described] cost you in [time/resource they named]?" Thirty-second silence. Then: "Actually, yeah. A lot more than that." They had walked themselves to the price justification. The rep didn't need to.

## Slide 7
**Headline:** 9:00 – 11:00 | Close + Next Steps
**Body copy:** "So — does it make sense to put together a proposal based on what we've covered?" Not "what do you think?" Not "are you interested?" A specific, low-friction yes/no question tied to the next concrete step. The answer: "Yes, I'd like to see that." They agreed to a follow-up call with their manager. The deal closed two weeks later.
