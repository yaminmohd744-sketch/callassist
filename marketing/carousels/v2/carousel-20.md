## Slide 2
**Headline:** Days 1–30: Shut up and listen.
**Body copy:** Your job in the first 30 days is not to perform. It's to understand. Listen to every call you can access — recordings, live, shadowed. Ask the manager and the top rep what they wish they'd known earlier. Don't pitch your ideas for improvement yet. Map how the team actually works vs. how it's supposed to work. Those are almost always different.

## Slide 3
**Headline:** The most important call to shadow is the top rep, not the best talker.
**Body copy:** Every team has someone who "seems" elite — loud, confident, big energy. Shadow them if you want. But the rep you want to study is the one who consistently hits quota with the least drama. They have a process. They're not winging it. They've built something repeatable and quiet. That's what you're learning to replicate.

## Slide 4
**Headline:** Days 1–30: Build your personal objection library.
**Body copy:** Every call you hear — live or recorded — write down every objection you notice. Not just the ones from the script. The real ones. The weird ones. The ones the rep handled badly. By the end of 30 days, you should have a list of 15–20 objections with at least two response approaches for each. This is your first competitive advantage.

## Slide 5
**Headline:** Days 31–60: Start running calls. Track everything.
**Body copy:** You're now on the phone. And your job is to generate data, not just dials. Track: your talk-to-listen ratio, where in the call you're losing people, which objections you're handling vs. dropping, which opening lines get you past 60 seconds. You don't need a fancy tool. A notes doc works. What matters is the review habit — every week without fail.

## Slide 6
**Headline:** Days 31–60: When to start personalising your script.
**Body copy:** Not before you've run 50 calls with the standard one. Scripts aren't cages — but they're important scaffolding while you're learning the structure. Once you've got 50 calls under your belt and can see where the script is working and where it's fighting you — that's when you start adapting. Not before. Premature personalisation is just excuses to improvise poorly.

## Slide 7
**Headline:** Days 61–90: Diagnose. Improve. Prove.
**Body copy:** You now have 60 days of data on yourself. Pull your numbers: conversion rate at each stage, average call length, close rate, most common objection type. Find the one metric that's furthest from the team average and build one specific improvement plan for it. This is the conversation to have with your manager at day 90 — not "I'm working hard." But: "Here's my stage-2 conversion rate. Here's what I'm doing to move it."

## Slide 8
**Headline:** How to not burn out in your first 90 days.
**Body copy:** The reps who last are not the ones who work the most hours in month one. They're the ones who set a hard stop at the end of the day — and use the hour before bed for something that has nothing to do with sales. Gym. Reading. Walking. Something physical. You cannot process the emotional weight of rejection without a reset routine. Build it before you need it.

**Headline:** The 90-day roadmap summarised.
**Body copy:** Days 1–30: Listen. Shadow. Build your objection library. Days 31–60: Run calls. Track everything. Learn the script before you break it. Days 61–90: Diagnose your numbers. **Clip structure:** One phase per cut — three main clips (0–30, 31–60, 61–90) or one full 60-second video
**Text overlays:** Phase label appears as a chapter card at the top, bullet points appear sequentially below
**Music vibe:** Warm, forward-moving — something that has optimism and energy without being a hype track

**Standalone clips:** The "personal objection library" slide (Day 1–30), the "personalize your script" timing rule, and the burnout prevention slide are all strong standalone TikToks
