## Slide 2
**Headline:** Objection #1: "We already have a solution."
**Body copy:**
DON'T say: "Oh, what are you using?"
DO say: "That makes sense — most of my clients were using [X] before they switched. What I've found is [specific gap]. Is that something you're running into?"

Re-frame it as curiosity, not competition.

**Visual direction:**
- Dark card (#111111) with #814ac8 left accent border
- "DON'T" label in #ff4444, "DO" label in #39d353
- Both response blocks in contrasting treatments — red muted, green highlighted

## Slide 3
**Headline:** Objection #2: "Send me some information."
**Body copy:**
DON'T say: "Sure! I'll send that over now."
DO say: "Happy to — what's the specific thing you'd want to understand better? That way I'll send exactly what's relevant and we can reconnect on [day]."

They're brushing you off. Requalify before you hit send.

**Visual direction:**
- Same card format as Slide 2 — consistent visual rhythm for the series
- "Send me information" in speech bubble above the card — dark #111111 bg, magenta border

---

## Slide 4
**Headline:** Objection #3: "We don't have the budget."
**Body copy:**
DON'T say: "We have flexible pricing."
DO say: "Totally fair — is budget the blocker, or is it more about whether this is the right priority right now? Because those are two different conversations."

Separate 'no money' from 'not convinced it's worth it.'

**Visual direction:**
- Same card format
- "$" symbol integrated subtly — #df7afe glyph in the corner of the card

---

## Slide 5
**Headline:** Objection #4: "Now's not a good time."
**Body copy:**
DON'T say: "When would be a better time?"
DO say: "I get it — when's the clock on [problem they mentioned]? Because if it's Q3, now might actually be exactly the right time to figure this out."

Tie it to urgency they already own, not urgency you're manufacturing.

**Visual direction:**
- Calendar icon (#814ac8) bottom corner of card
- "Now's not a good time." speech bubble above in same dark card style

---

## Slide 6
**Headline:** Objection #5: "Just email me."
**Body copy:**
DON'T say: "Of course, what's your email?"
DO say: "I'd rather not waste your inbox — what would make it worth 10 minutes on a call? [pause] Because everyone I've sent a deck to who never replied ended up having the same 2-question conversation I could have had in 5 minutes."

Be direct. Ask for what you want.

**Visual direction:**
- Email icon crossed out lightly — metaphor for refusing the brush-off
- Card in same format, consistent rhythm
