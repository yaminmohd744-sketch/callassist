## Slide 2
**Headline:** Mistake 1 — You never confirmed the decision maker is on the call.
**Body copy:** You ran a 35-minute discovery call. The person was engaged. Then they said, "I'll need to run it by my manager." That call was a rehearsal, not a discovery. Always confirm authority before you invest 30+ minutes. If they can't say yes, find out who can — and get them on the call.

**Do this instead:** "Just to make sure I'm giving you the full picture — who else would be involved in a decision like this?"

## Slide 3
**Headline:** Mistake 2 — You asked surface questions.
**Body copy:** "What are your current challenges?" is a surface question. "If this problem isn't solved in the next 90 days, what happens to your team?" is an impact question. Surface questions get surface answers. Impact questions reveal the emotional and financial weight of the problem — the real fuel for every close.

**Do this instead:** For every challenge a prospect mentions, ask: "What's the downstream effect of that on your team/number/quarter?"

**Visual direction:**
- Background: #0a0a0a
- Two-column contrast: "Surface question" (grey, shallow) vs "Impact question" (#814ac8, deep) — represented as two wells of different depths
- Example questions labelled inside each well as text
- "Mistake 2 of 5" pill

## Slide 4
**Headline:** Mistake 3 — You pitched in discovery.
**Body copy:** They described a problem. You saw a match to your product. You said "Oh, actually we do that — here's how it works." And the discovery call became a demo. Now you've shown the solution before you've understood the full picture, and the prospect has no reason to come back for a formal presentation.

**Do this instead:** When you see a match, hold it. Write it down. Say: "That's exactly the kind of thing I want to make sure we address — let me make a note of that." Save the solution for the demo.

## Slide 5
**Headline:** Mistake 4 — You didn't set a next step before the call ended.
**Body copy:** You wrapped up with "Great chatting — I'll send over some info and we can find a time." That's not a next step. That's a hope. A next step has a date, a time, an attendee list, and a confirmed purpose. If you don't book it before you hang up, your chances of booking it after drop by over 50%.

**Do this instead:** "Before we wrap — does Tuesday at 2pm work for a 30-minute deep-dive with [name]? I'll send the invite right now."

## Slide 6
**Headline:** Mistake 5 — You talked about features instead of outcomes.
**Body copy:** "We have a real-time transcription engine with sentiment analysis and NLP-powered objection detection." That's a feature. "We've seen reps close 30% more deals in their first month" — that's an outcome. Prospects don't buy features. They buy futures. They buy the version of their job that's easier, faster, or less stressful.

**Do this instead:** Every feature you mention, follow it with "which means for your team…" and complete the sentence with a specific outcome.
