## Slide 3
**Headline:** Technique 1 — Mirroring.
**Body copy:** Repeat the last 1–3 words a prospect just said, slightly upward in tone. That's it. It keeps them talking, signals you're listening, and surfaces new information you can use. Voss called mirroring "the simplest tool in the negotiator's kit."

**Example:**
Prospect: "We're not really in the market right now."
You: "Not in the market right now?"
Prospect: [explains their actual situation]

## Slide 4
**Headline:** Technique 2 — Labelling emotions.
**Body copy:** Name what the prospect is feeling, without confirming or denying it. Labelling defuses emotional resistance and makes the prospect feel genuinely heard. Use "It seems like…" or "It sounds like…" — never "I feel like you're…" which makes it about you.

**Example:**
"It seems like timing is a real concern here."
"It sounds like you've had a bad experience with vendors before."

## Slide 5
**Headline:** Technique 3 — Calibrated questions.
**Body copy:** Voss banned yes/no questions. Instead: "How" and "What" questions that give the prospect a sense of control while moving the conversation forward. These questions also buy you time and reveal the real objection under the stated one.

**Example:**
Instead of "Can we move forward?" → "What would need to be true for this to work for your team?"
Instead of "Is price the issue?" → "How are you typically thinking about budget for this kind of solution?"

## Slide 6
**Headline:** Put all three together on a real call.
**Body copy:** Prospect says: "This isn't really a priority right now."

Mirror → "Not a priority right now?"
Label → "It sounds like there's a lot competing for your attention."
Calibrated Q → "What would need to change for this to move up the list?"

Now they're talking. Now you know what the real objection is.

## Slide 7
**Headline:** Why this works in sales when nothing else does.
**Body copy:** Prospects are not hostile. But they are guarded. They've heard 50 pitches. Their defences are up before you open your mouth. Tactical empathy bypasses the defence entirely — it doesn't push, it pulls. The prospect moves themselves toward the close.

**Body copy:**
