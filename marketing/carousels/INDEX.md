## Recommended Posting Order

Post in this sequence to maximise saves, shares, and warm the audience before introducing the product directly.

### Phase 1 — Value first (Weeks 1–2)
Start with the highest save-rate content: scripts and stats. These carousels earn follows and saves from cold audiences who don't know Pitchr yet. No product mention in the hook. Pure utility.

| Week | Post | Why |
|------|------|-----|
| Week 1, Post 1 | **Carousel 05** — "It's Too Expensive" Scripts | Highest immediate utility — everyone faces this objection daily. Top save candidate. |
| Week 1, Post 2 | **Carousel 03** — The 7 Stats | Data carousels get shared internally and tagged in. Builds credibility. |
| Week 2, Post 1 | **Carousel 06** — "Let Me Think About It" Scripts | Companion to C05 — captures the audience who saved C05 and wants more. |
| Week 2, Post 2 | **Carousel 04** — Top 1% of Closers (Data) | Aspiration + data. Share and save magnet for AEs and SDRs. |

### Phase 2 — Authority and education (Weeks 3–4)
Introduce the "Famous Closers" and "Psychology" carousels. These build brand authority and attract a more curious, engaged audience. Repost rates tend to be high for intellectual content in sales communities.

| Week | Post | Why |
|------|------|-----|
| Week 3, Post 1 | **Carousel 01** — Jordan Belfort's System | High name recognition. Aspiration content. Draws in the motivated reps. |
| Week 3, Post 2 | **Carousel 09** — Cialdini's 6 Triggers | Educational depth. Saved by managers and team leads as a training resource. |
| Week 4, Post 1 | **Carousel 02** — Chris Voss Tactical Empathy | FBI angle is a scroll-stopper. Curiosity-driven shares. |
| Week 4, Post 2 | **Carousel 10** — Loss Aversion in Sales | Pairs with C09. The intellectual duo builds authority positioning for Pitchr. |

### Phase 3 — Diagnosis and mistakes (Week 5)
Now the audience knows you deliver value. Introduce the "Common Mistakes" carousels — slightly confrontational content that generates comments and self-tagging. High engagement rate.

| Week | Post | Why |
|------|------|-----|
| Week 5, Post 1 | **Carousel 07** — 7 Mistakes Killing Your Close Rate | Highest comment-generating format — people self-identify publicly. |
| Week 5, Post 2 | **Carousel 08** — Discovery Calls Not Converting | More specific audience (AE-focused). High save rate from managers who share with teams. |

### Phase 4 — Product introduction (Week 6)
By week 6, the audience trusts the account for sales intelligence. Now introduce Pitchr through transformation content — not features. The product carousels feel earned, not forced.

| Week | Post | Why |
|------|------|-----|
| Week 6, Post 1 | **Carousel 11** — Monday Without vs. With Pitchr | Concrete, relatable, data-driven before/after. Lower barrier than the emotional carousel. |
| Week 6, Post 2 | **Carousel 12** — The Call That Changed Everything | Narrative closer — highest emotional resonance. End on this. It's the one people finish and share. |

## Notes on Sequencing

- **Never open with product carousels.** Pitchr is unknown to most viewers in week 1. Lead with value that stands alone.
- **Scripts carousels (C05, C06) have the highest save rate** of any format in B2B sales content — they belong at the front.
- **Stats carousels (C03, C04) have the highest share and tag rate** — they bring in new followers organically.
- **Mistakes carousels (C07, C08) drive comments** — people respond defensively or confessionally. Both drive reach.
- **Psychology carousels (C09, C10) attract managers and team leads** who save content to share in team meetings. These grow a higher-quality follower base.
- **Product carousels (C11, C12) convert** — but only to an audience that already values the account.
- **Carousel 12 is the brand anchor piece.** It should run last in the initial sequence but can be reposted as a hero piece once the account has an audience.
- All 12 carousels can loop — the sequence can repeat with updated examples, fresh stats, or seasonal variations.
