## Slide 2
**Headline:** Mistake 1 — Pitching before you understand the pain.
**Body copy:** You launch into the product within the first 2 minutes. The prospect hasn't told you what they actually need — and you're already talking about features. A pitch without a pain is just noise. Stop pitching. Start diagnosing.

**Do this instead:** Ask three open questions before you say anything about your product. "What's the biggest challenge you're trying to solve right now?"

**Visual direction:**
- Background: #111111
- Left column: the mistake in a red-tinted dark card (#1a1a1a, #ff4444 border) — bold, 3 lines
- Right column: the fix in a green-tinted card (#1a1a1a, #39d353 border) — bold, 3 lines
- "Mistake 1 of 7" pill, top-right in #ff4444

## Slide 3
**Headline:** Mistake 2 — Not confirming the budget.
**Body copy:** You make it all the way to the proposal stage without knowing if they can afford it. Then you lose 3 weeks of selling time when they come back with "our budget is $X." Budget questions feel awkward. Missing quota feels worse.

**Do this instead:** "Just so we're building the right proposal — what's the typical investment range for a solution like this for your team?"

**Visual direction:**
- Background: #111111
- Same mistake/fix two-column card layout
- "$$$" icon with a question mark in the mistake card — red tint

- "Mistake 2 of 7" pill

## Slide 4
**Headline:** Mistake 3 — Talking more than listening.
**Body copy:** You're at 72% talk time. You think you're giving information. You're actually eliminating opportunities to learn what will close the deal. Every sentence you speak is a sentence where you're not discovering what the prospect cares about.

**Do this instead:** Set a personal rule — let the prospect finish every thought before responding. Count the gap. Sit in it.

**Visual direction:**
- Background: #0a0a0a
- Microphone icon (oversized, #ff4444) representing the rep vs small ear icon (#39d353) representing listening
- Talk/listen bar: 72% talk (red) vs 28% listen (green) — as a visual ratio
- "Mistake 3 of 7" pill

## Slide 5
**Headline:** Mistake 4 — Missing buying signals.
**Body copy:** They said "how long does onboarding take?" — and you moved on. They said "does this integrate with Salesforce?" — and you gave a one-word answer. Buying signals are questions about implementation. When a prospect asks about logistics, they're already mentally in the deal.

**Do this instead:** When you hear a buying signal, lean in: "That's a great question — tell me more about what your current setup looks like."

**Visual direction:**
- Background: #111111
- Example buying signal bubbles in a soft speech-bubble format: "How long does onboarding take?" / "Does it work with our CRM?" / "What's the support like?"
- Each bubble in a #814ac8-tinted card — labelled "BUYING SIGNAL" in small green caps
- "Mistake 4 of 7" pill

## Slide 6
**Headline:** Mistake 5 — Not asking for the close.
**Body copy:** You had a great call. You built rapport. You answered every question. And then you said "I'll send over the info and we can catch up soon." You didn't ask. If you never ask, the answer is always no by default.

**Do this instead:** "Based on everything we've discussed, does it make sense for us to take the next step?"

**Visual direction:**
- Background: #0a0a0a
- Large visual: two contrasting paths — "Great call, no ask" → [pipeline ghosts] vs "Great call + close question" → [booked deal icon]
- Script line for the close question in the #f0ebff card
- "Mistake 5 of 7" pill

## Slide 7
**Headline:** Mistake 6 — Over-explaining the product.
**Body copy:** The prospect asked "how does it work?" You responded with a 6-minute feature deep dive. Now they're overwhelmed and confused, not excited. Features don't close deals. Outcomes do. Every feature you explain should be tied to a result they care about.

**Do this instead:** "The way it works is [one sentence]. The reason that matters for your situation is [specific outcome]."

**Visual direction:**
- Background: #111111
- Long winding path visual representing over-explanation vs a short direct path representing the outcome-first approach
- Short path highlighted in #814ac8, long path in #444444 winding off the edge
- "Mistake 6 of 7" pill

## Slide 8
**Headline:** Mistake 7 — Accepting "no" too quickly.
**Body copy:** They said "we're not interested." You said "no problem, thanks for your time." 80% of prospects say no before they say yes. One no is not a rejection — it's the start of the conversation. The rep who hears a no and asks one more question closes more than anyone else in the room.

**Do this instead:** "I appreciate that — out of curiosity, what would need to change for this to be worth a second look down the line?"

**Visual direction:**

- Large "NO" in red (#ff4444) crossed out — replaced by "NOT YET" in #df7afe
- Script line for the follow-up question below in the #f0ebff card
