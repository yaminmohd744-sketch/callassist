## Slide 2
**Headline:** Response 1 — Ask What Specifically
**Body copy:**
**Say this:** "Of course — what specifically is it that you need to think through? I want to make sure I've given you everything you need."

**Why it works:** "Let me think about it" is almost always a cover for an unexpressed concern. This question opens the door — gently. Most prospects will tell you the real objection once you ask directly. You can't handle an objection you don't know about.

**When to use:** First response, every time. Always clarify before you do anything else.

## Slide 3
**Headline:** Response 2 — The Future-Pace Technique
**Body copy:**
**Say this:** "Totally understand. Imagine it's six months from now and you went ahead with this — what would that have meant for your team?"

**Why it works:** Gets the prospect mentally living with the solution already. Future-pacing activates desire rather than resistance. They're no longer evaluating a decision — they're experiencing the outcome. That's a very different conversation.

**When to use:** After you've already confirmed the pain is real and the fit is strong. Don't future-pace if discovery isn't done.

## Slide 4
**Headline:** Response 3 — The Summary Close
**Body copy:**
**Say this:** "Before we pause — let me just recap what we've covered. You said [pain point 1], [pain point 2], and [goal]. And we've shown that this directly addresses all three. Is there anything in that summary that doesn't feel right?"

**Why it works:** Forces agreement on the logic step by step before the final decision. If they agree with every element of the summary, the "think about it" becomes harder to sustain. They've already confirmed the fit — they're just not committing yet.

**When to use:** When you've had a strong discovery and the pain is well-established.

## Slide 5
**Headline:** Response 4 — The Take-Away Close
**Body copy:**
**Say this:** "No worries at all. Just so I know — if you do decide to move forward, are we talking about the standard package or would you want to include [add-on]? I ask because onboarding slots are filling up for this month."

**Why it works:** The moment you take away the option, scarcity becomes real. By asking about configuration rather than commitment, you assume forward movement. If they engage with the question, they're mentally already moving. If they disengage, you've learned something.

**When to use:** When there is a genuine capacity or timing constraint you can reference. Never manufacture fake urgency.

## Slide 6
**Headline:** Response 5 — The Timeline Question
**Body copy:**
**Say this:** "Absolutely — when do you think you'll have a sense of where things are? I want to respect your process and also make sure we're set up to move quickly when you're ready."

**Why it works:** Gets a concrete date on the calendar without pressure. "Think about it" becomes a defined follow-up conversation. You now have a specific time to re-engage — and the prospect has committed to a next step, even if they don't realise it.

**When to use:** When none of the previous responses opened a conversation — use this as the graceful exit that still preserves the deal.

## Slide 7
**Headline:** The real problem with "let me think about it."
**Body copy:** It's not a no. It's not a yes. It's a pause that, without intervention, defaults to a no. Every day that passes, your deal loses heat. Momentum is everything — and momentum dies when you say "sounds good, I'll follow up." Your job is to keep the conversation alive.
