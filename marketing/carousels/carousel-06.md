## Slide 2
**Headline:** The #1 reason deals die in discovery: the rep started selling too soon.
**Body copy:** Before the prospect feels understood, they can't be sold. They'll sit through your pitch, say "interesting," and go dark. Discovery isn't the setup for the pitch — it IS the pitch.

**Visual direction:**
- Timeline: "Rep pitches at minute 3" marked with a red X vs. "Prospect feels understood at minute 15" marked with a green check
- Dark background #0a0a0a, timeline in #814ac8 line

## Slide 3
**Headline:** Question 1: "What made you take this call today?"
**Body copy:** This reveals buying intent and the triggering event. Everything else you ask should connect back to what they say here. It's your map for the whole conversation.

**Visual direction:**
- Question number "1" in large #814ac8 numeral — top left of dark card
- Question text in large #f0ebff, bold
- Insight annotation below in smaller text (#f0ebff 65% opacity): "This is your map."

---

## Slide 4
**Headline:** Question 2: "What's the current situation and what's the cost of leaving it as is?"
**Body copy:** Don't guess the pain — make them articulate it. The prospect needs to hear themselves say the problem out loud. That's what creates urgency.

**Visual direction:**
- Same numbered card format — "2" in #814ac8
- "Make them say it out loud." as the annotation in #df7afe italic

---

## Slide 5
**Headline:** Question 3: "What have you already tried?"
**Body copy:** This shows you respect their intelligence — they've already been thinking about this. It also reveals why previous solutions failed, which is your opening.

**Visual direction:**
- "3" card format consistent with prior slides
- "This is your opening." annotation in #39d353 — positive signal

---

## Slide 6
**Headline:** Questions 4–6: The close-setup trio.
**Body copy:**
4. "If you could wave a magic wand and fix one thing — what would it be?"
5. "What would need to be true to make a decision by [timeframe]?"
6. "Who else needs to be part of this decision?"

These three questions surface the dream outcome, the decision timeline, and the stakeholder map — everything you need to close.

**Visual direction:**
- Three mini-cards stacked vertically in a column — each with a number badge (#814ac8 circle)
- Questions in #f0ebff, annotations below each in #df7afe smaller text
- Background shifts to show the full layout cleanly
