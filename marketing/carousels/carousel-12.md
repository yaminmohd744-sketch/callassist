## Slide 4
**Headline:** The first five minutes went fine.
**Body copy:** Marcus had prepped. He knew the prospect's company, their quarter, their headcount. He opened well. The prospect was engaged. And then, twenty minutes in, everything changed.

"Marcus, I have to be honest with you. We've seen something similar from a competitor and the price is 40% lower. Walk me through why I should pay more."

Marcus had never been asked this so directly. His mind went blank.

## Slide 7

**Body copy:** "I hear you on the price — that's a fair thing to raise. Can I ask: what does their implementation and onboarding look like? Our customers typically find that the first 90 days determine whether the cheaper option was actually cheaper."

"That's... actually a good point. We hadn't thought about that."

## TikTok Adaptation Notes
- This carousel is a short film. Voiceover should be calm, narrative, measured — not fast, not hype
- Slide 1: Title card energy. Pause for a beat before starting. Music is present but barely.
- Slides 2–3: Scene-setting. Voiceover is almost a whisper — drawing the viewer in
- Slide 4: Pace changes. The objection is read slowly, deliberately. Then: "His mind went blank." — full stop, pause, let the viewer sit in it
- Slide 5: "Uncertainty is contagious." — deliver this quietly. Not a lecture. An observation.
- Slide 6: The Pitchr prompt appears on screen. Voiceover reads it word for word. Slightly faster — this is the turn.
- Slide 7: Read the dialogue with two voices. Marcus — steady, controlled. Prospect — slightly surprised.
- Slides 8–9: Resolution builds. Music begins to warm. Tone lifts.
- Slide 10: Direct address. "What would you do differently on your next call?" — let it sit for a full second
- CTA: Music resolves. The call to action is quiet and confident — not urgent
- Music vibe: Cinematic ambient — something like Hans Zimmer-adjacent, but minimal. Not dramatic. Just present.
- Pacing: 25–35 seconds total — the longest carousel in the series by design. This is the one people finish.
