## Slide 2
**Headline:** Trigger 1 — Reciprocity
**Body copy:** When you give something valuable before asking for anything, people feel a subconscious obligation to give back. In sales: send a relevant piece of insight before the call. Share a benchmark report. Point them to something useful, unprompted. Give value before you ever pitch — and the door opens.

**On your next call:** "Before we dive in, I put together a quick benchmark for your industry — I thought it might be useful regardless of whether we work together."

## Slide 3
**Headline:** Trigger 2 — Commitment & Consistency
**Body copy:** Once someone publicly commits to a small step, they are psychologically motivated to stay consistent with that commitment. In sales: get small agreements throughout the call — "Does that make sense so far?" / "Is that the kind of outcome you're looking for?" — before you ever ask for the close.

**On your next call:** Build micro-agreements through the call. The close is just the final agreement in a chain of yeses.

## Slide 4
**Headline:** Trigger 3 — Social Proof
**Body copy:** People follow people who are like them. Buyers look to other buyers for permission to say yes. Name-drop relevant customers — ideally in the same industry, same role, same pain. "We work with 200 SaaS sales teams in your size range, and the most common result in month one is…"

**On your next call:** Always have a story ready from a customer who looks like your prospect. Specificity multiplies credibility.

## Slide 5
**Headline:** Trigger 4 — Authority
**Body copy:** Prospects buy from experts, not vendors. Authority is signalled through language, specificity, and what you know. Reps who know the industry deeply, cite data, and reference trends command 2x the credibility of reps who only know the product. Become the expert on your customer's world — not just your solution.

**On your next call:** Open with an industry insight they might not know. "I was looking at some benchmarks for your space before this call — one thing that stood out was…"

## Slide 6
**Headline:** Trigger 5 — Liking
**Body copy:** People buy from people they like. Liking is not about being entertaining — it's about similarity, genuine interest, and remembered details. Mirror their communication style. Remember what they mentioned in the last call. Ask about the project they mentioned. Small acts of attention create disproportionate trust.

**On your next call:** Reference something from a previous conversation or their LinkedIn — not to impress them, but to show you actually paid attention.

**Visual direction:**

## Slide 7
**Headline:** Trigger 6 — Scarcity
**Body copy:** The perceived value of anything increases when availability decreases. But fake scarcity destroys trust instantly. Real scarcity — implementation slots, pricing tiers, team capacity, quarter-end cutoffs — is a legitimate urgency driver. Scarcity must be true, specific, and relevant to their situation.

**On your next call:** "We have two implementation slots remaining in Q2 and my onboarding team is at capacity after that — I don't want you to miss the window if this is the right fit."

**Visual direction:**
