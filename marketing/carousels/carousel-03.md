**Body copy:** 44% of salespeople give up after one follow-up. The average deal requires 5 to 8 touches to close. Most reps never get there — and they blame the prospect.

*(Source: Marketing Donut / HubSpot research)*

**Visual direction:**
- Background: #0a0a0a
- "44%" in Figtree ExtraBold at 120px, gradient (#814ac8 → #df7afe), centred at top
- Body copy in #c4b8e0 at 17px below, left-aligned

- "Stat 1 of 7" label in #814ac8 small pill, top-right

## Slide 3
**Headline:** 92%
**Body copy:** 92% of sales professionals give up before the fourth "no." But 80% of prospects say no four times before they say yes. The persistence gap is where most deals die.

*(Source: National Sales Executive Association)*

**Visual direction:**
- Background: #0a0a0a
- "92%" in same gradient at 120px
- Body copy and source in #c4b8e0 at 17px
- Visual contrast element: two columns — "Reps who quit" (92%) in red-tinted bar vs "Prospects who eventually say yes" (80%) in green bar
- "Stat 2 of 7" pill label

## Slide 4
**Headline:** 47%
**Body copy:** 47% of lost deals are lost to "no decision" — not to a competitor. The prospect chose to do nothing. That means the rep failed to create enough urgency or make the cost of inaction clear. You didn't lose to anyone. You lost to the status quo.

*(Source: Salesforce State of Sales)*

**Visual direction:**
- Background: #111111
- "47%" in gradient numerals at 120px
- Stat breakdown beneath: "Lost to competitor: 53% / Lost to no decision: 47%" — minimal two-segment pie icon
- Bold callout line in #df7afe: "You didn't lose to a competitor. You lost to inaction."
- "Stat 3 of 7" pill label

## Slide 5
**Headline:** 7 seconds.
**Body copy:** Prospects form an initial impression of a sales rep within 7 seconds of the call starting. Tone, pace, and energy in the opener determine whether the next 10 minutes happen at all. The pitch doesn't matter if you lost them at "Hey, is this a good time?"

*(Source: Various vocal persuasion research)*

**Visual direction:**
- Background: #000000
- "7 seconds." in gradient at 100px — slightly smaller to allow the emotional body copy more space
- Clock/stopwatch icon in #814ac8 next to the numeral
- Body copy at 16px in #c4b8e0
- Italic callout in #df7afe: "'Hey, is this a good time?' — the worst opener in sales."
- "Stat 4 of 7" pill

*(Source: Sales Benchmark Index / HubSpot)*

## TikTok Adaptation Notes
- Slide 1: "7 stats that explain why you're losing deals. None of them are about your product." — delivered with no filler, confident, slightly provocative
- Slides 2–8: Each stat is its own beat — voiceover says the number first, pauses, then explains in 1–2 sentences
- Slide 5 (7 seconds): Deliver this with urgency — "Seven. Seconds." — pause — "That's how long you have before the call is won or lost."
- Slide 7 (57% missed quota): Softer tone — this one hits personally for many viewers
- Slide 9: Reframe slide — tone shifts from diagnostic to empowering
- CTA: Confident close
- Music vibe: building electronic, slightly tense — escalates stat by stat
- Pacing: 18–22 seconds total, punchy on stat numbers, reflective on the commentary
