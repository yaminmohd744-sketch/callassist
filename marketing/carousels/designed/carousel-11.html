Why they stop

Why most reps stop at 2.

It feels rude to follow up a third time. It feels desperate at four. Nobody trained you on what to say on touch 6. And when the prospect goes quiet, the silence reads like rejection — so you protect yourself by giving up first.

Meanwhile, your prospect is just busy.

2 / 9
Pitchr
Touch 1 — Day 0

The first follow-up:
confirm and add value.

✓

Recap their words (not yours)

What mattered most to them — in their language

✓

One relevant resource

Specific to the problem they named

✓

One clear ask

A specific next step, not a vague "let me know"

Time it 2 hours post-call. Move fast. ⚡

Pitchr
Touch 2 — Day 3

A new data point,
not a nudge.

“Just checking in”

Says: I have nothing new. Please respond anyway.

Instead: share something new.

A case study from their industry. An insight from a similar conversation. A stat they'd actually care about. Make them glad they opened it.

4 / 9
Pitchr
Touch 3 — Day 7

Challenge their
status quo.

By touch 3, they've either forgotten you or are avoiding you. Either way — pattern interrupt.

The question to ask

“If [problem] stays unresolved for the next 90 days, what does that cost you?”

It restarts the conversation by restarting the pain.

Touch 8+ — The break-up email

The email with the highest
response rate.

“I've reached out a few times without hearing back. I don't want to clutter your inbox if timing isn't right — so this is my last note unless you want to reconnect. No pressure, no hard feelings. If things change: [your email].”

Higher response rate than touches 3–7 combined.
