The truth about sales

They told me it was easy money.

Nobody mentioned this part.

1 / 10
Pitchr
1

Nobody told you rejection has a physical feeling.

The moment before you dial a number you don't want to call — that tension in your chest is real. It doesn't mean you're weak. It means you're human.

The best reps feel it too. They've just stopped letting it make the decision.

2 / 10
Pitchr
2

It takes longer to get good than anyone admits.

"You'll ramp in 60 days." Sure. But real fluency — where you can handle any objection, read any prospect, and control a call — that takes 18 months minimum.

Nobody says this because it doesn't help with hiring.

3 / 10
Pitchr
3

Most sales training doesn't work on real calls.

The roleplay is perfect. Your script is memorised. Then someone asks "why should I switch from what I'm using now?" and...

Your brain empties.

Training builds knowledge. Repetition on live calls builds skill. They're not the same thing.

📚 ≠ 📞
4 / 10
Pitchr
4

Activity and results are not the same thing.

100 calls a day sounds impressive. But if you're calling the wrong people, leading with the wrong message, and never reviewing what's working — you're just building scar tissue, not skill.

"Practice at being average."

5 / 10
Pitchr
5

The emotional toll is real and nobody budgets for it.

Getting told no 40 times before lunch affects you. Even when you rationally know it's not personal.

The reps who last aren't the ones without feelings — they're the ones with a routine for managing them.

Gym · Walk · Call debrief
6 / 10
Pitchr
6

The rep who "makes it look easy" has a system.

They're not calmer. They're not more naturally charming. They've mapped their objections. They've built their own playbook. They know the five most likely paths a call will take.

That's not talent. That's preparation.

7 / 10
Pitchr

None of this is to scare you.

Sales is still one of the fastest ways to build an income, a network, and a set of transferable skills that work everywhere.

But knowing the real version — not the pitch deck version — is what separates reps who last from reps who burn out at 9 months.

8 / 10
Pitchr
7

Volume without feedback is just practice at being average.

The top 1% treat every lost call as a dataset. That's how they compound their skill while everyone else just logs dials.
