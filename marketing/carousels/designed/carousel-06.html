Pitchr
🚩 Red Flag

"We have an amazing culture here."

Translation: The base pay is below market and we're hoping vibes compensate. Always ask: what's the base? What's the realistic OTE based on actual rep performance — not top performers?

Culture doesn't pay rent.

2 / 10
Pitchr
🚩 Red Flag

"Uncapped commission!"

On a $20 average order value. Or with an 8-month sales cycle and a 3% close rate. Uncapped commission is only meaningful if the mechanics underneath it are real.

Ask: What did the median rep make in commission last year?

3 / 10
Pitchr
🚩 Red Flag

"We'll train you — don't worry."

Translation: we have a 3-day Zoom onboarding, a shared Google Drive, and a manager who expects you to figure it out. Ask: is there a defined 30/60/90? Do you shadow calls before you run them?

That's on you to build from scratch.

4 / 10
Pitchr
🚩 Red Flag

The hiring manager can't tell you who their best rep is.

If they can't immediately name a top performer and explain what makes them effective — there's no coaching culture. Good sales organisations celebrate their best reps openly and are specific about why they win.

Vagueness about performance is a signal.

5 / 10
Pitchr
✅ Green Flag

They can describe the ICP in two sentences.

"We sell to VP of Operations at mid-market logistics companies, 200–1,000 employees, dealing with routing inefficiency." If they can say that clearly — they know who they're selling to.

Your pipeline won't be full of accidental meetings.

6 / 10
Pitchr
✅ Green Flag

The manager was a top rep — and still coaches actively.

The best sales managers were excellent individual contributors who chose to grow others. If your manager can jump on a call with you and demonstrate — not just advise — you'll learn faster than any training programme.

Demonstrate — not just advise.

7 / 10
Pitchr
✅ Green Flag

There's a real tech stack and a CRM everyone actually uses.

Salesforce with actual data in it. A dialer. A sequencing tool. If reps are logging calls in spreadsheets and "we're implementing Salesforce soon" — the ops infrastructure doesn't exist.

No data, no coaching, no accountability.

8 / 10
Pitchr

5 questions to ask in every sales interview.

1.What % of reps hit OTE last year?
2.What's the average ramp time?
3.Can I speak to a current rep — not chosen by the recruiter?
4.What does the first 90 days look like in writing?
5.What percentage of reps hit quota last year?

Screenshot this. Use it before you sign anything.
