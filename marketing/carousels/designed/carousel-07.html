SECONDS 0–3

Don't explain who you are first.

"Hi, this is [Name] from [Company] — do you have a few minutes?"

This gives the prospect nothing to care about before asking them to commit.

Lead with relevance, not identity.

2
LEAD WITH A PATTERN INTERRUPT

Trigger curiosity, not the defensive reflex.

"Hey [Name], you're probably going to want to hang up in about 20 seconds — but give me that long."

Sounds confident. Sounds human. Triggers curiosity instead of the auto-defensive reflex.

SECONDS 4–10

Give them a reason to stay on.

"I work with [role type] at [company type] dealing with [specific problem]. If that's not relevant, I'll let you go — but if it is, it's worth 5 minutes."
1. Know them 2. Name the problem 3. Lower the stakes

4
SECONDS 11–20

Earn curiosity, not commitment.

You're not trying to pitch the product here. You're trying to get them to agree the problem is real.

"Is [specific problem] something you're actively dealing with, or has it not been a priority yet?"

Open a door. Don't close a deal.

5
SECONDS 21–30

Set the agenda or earn the objection.

"Takes about 15 minutes — I'll ask a few questions, share what I'm seeing with similar teams, and if it's not a fit I'll tell you."

Confident, respectful, removes pressure.

The agenda is the close.

The 4 words that kill every cold call opening.

"Is now a bad time?"

It's a yes/no where "yes" ends the call and "no" means you've asked permission rather than earned it.

Never ask this. Assume the call is worth their time. The energy comes through.
