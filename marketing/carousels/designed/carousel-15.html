0:00 – 0:30 | Opening

The Opening

The rep didn't apologise for calling. Didn't ask if it was a good time. Said:

“Hey [Name] — I know this is cold, but I've got a specific reason for reaching out. Give me 30 seconds and I'll tell you why.”

From a position of mutual respect,
not begged permission.

0:30 – 2:00 | Permission + Agenda

Permission + Agenda

Two things happened: they gave a reason to stay on, and made it feel collaborative instead of adversarial.

“I work with [role] at companies like yours dealing with [problem]. If it's relevant, I'd like to ask a few questions — takes about 10 minutes, and I'll tell you honestly if I think we can help. Sound fair?”

Collaborative instead of adversarial.

2:00 – 5:00 | Discovery

Discovery — Three Questions. No More.

1

“What does your current process look like?”

2

“Where does it break down?”

3

“What have you tried so far?”

The prospect had sold themselves on the problem.

5:00 – 7:00 | Presentation

Presentation — Two Minutes. A Mirror.

Not a feature dump. They demoed the two things the prospect had described as their exact pain points.

“Based on what you told me, the two things that sound most relevant are [X] and [Y]. Let me show you those specifically.”

“That's exactly what we need.”

The moment the deal turned.

7:00 – 9:00 | Objection Handling

Objection Handling

“The price is a bit higher than we expected.”

↓

“Totally fair — let me ask you something. Over the last year, what has [this problem] cost you in time and resources?”

They walked themselves to the price justification. The rep didn't need to.

9:00 – 11:00 | Close + Next Steps

Close + Next Steps

Not "what do you think?" Not "are you interested?" A specific, low-friction yes/no question:

“So — does it make sense to put together a proposal based on what we've covered?”

The deal closed two weeks later.

What made it perfect
wasn't the words.

It was the structure. Every phase had a job. Every question earned the next one.

The prospect did most of the talking — and left the call feeling heard, not sold to.
