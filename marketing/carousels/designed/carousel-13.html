Why objections are never what they sound like.

An objection is a door that's slightly open. "Too expensive" is not a financial statement — it's an emotional one. The prospect is telling you they haven't yet connected your price to their pain.

The objection is a request for more value.

The reps who understand this never argue about price.

Pitchr

Objection 1

They say

“It's too expensive.”

They mean

“I don't see the value clearly enough yet.” They're comparing your price to their current understanding — not to their budget.

Your move

“What would it need to do for the price to feel justified?”

3 / 10
Pitchr

Objection 2

They say

“I need to think about it.”

They mean

“I'm not convinced — but I don't want conflict.” This is the most common and most avoided objection.

Your move

“What specifically is making you hesitant? Is it the timing, the budget, or something about the fit?”

Name the thing they're avoiding.

4 / 10
Pitchr

Objection 3

They say

“Send me some information.”

They mean

“I want to get off the phone without being rude.” This is not an invitation — it's a polite hang-up.

Your move

“Of course — what specific part would be most useful? I want to make sure it's relevant to what you mentioned earlier.”

Do not send a PDF.

5 / 10
Pitchr

Objection 4

They say

“We already have a solution.”

They mean

“I don't understand what makes you different.” They're not dismissing you — they haven't seen your differentiation yet.

Your move

“What does your current solution do well? And where does it fall short?” Let them name the gap. Then show them exactly where you live.

Let them name the gap.

6 / 10
Pitchr

Objection 5

They say

“Now isn't a good time.”

They mean

“This isn't a priority right now.” Timing objections aren't about time — they're about urgency. The problem hasn't become painful enough to act on yet.

Your move

“What would need to change in the next 90 days for this to become a priority?” You're understanding what creates the trigger.

The pattern underneath every objection.

Every objection has the same structure: they're protecting themselves from a decision they haven't yet justified. Your job is never to argue, dismiss, or bulldoze.

Understand what they need to feel safe making the decision.

Discovery doesn't end at the pitch. It continues through every objection.

The one question that handles
almost every objection.

“Help me understand — what's making you hesitant about moving forward?”

The close was right there — on the other side of the question.
