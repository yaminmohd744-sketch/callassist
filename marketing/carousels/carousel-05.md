## Slide 2
**Headline:** Response 1 — The Curiosity Reframe
**Body copy:**
**Say this:** "Compared to what?"

**Why it works:** "Too expensive" is always relative. This one question makes the prospect articulate their benchmark — and half the time, they can't. It disrupts the objection without arguing. Silence is your friend after you say it.

**When to use:** When the objection feels reflexive rather than considered.

**Visual direction:**

- Three labelled sections below the card: WHY IT WORKS / WHEN TO USE — in #814ac8 small caps headers, #c4b8e0 body
- "Response 1 of 5" pill label, top-right

## Slide 3
**Headline:** Response 2 — The ROI Math
**Body copy:**
**Say this:** "What's it costing you not to solve this right now?"

**Why it works:** Shifts the frame from price-as-cost to price-as-investment. Forces the prospect to do the mental math on inaction. If their problem costs $20k a month and your solution is $2k, the math closes itself.

**When to use:** When you've already surfaced a quantifiable pain point in discovery.

**Visual direction:**
- Background: #111111
- Script card with the line in #f0ebff, large and centred
- Below: a simple equation visual — "Monthly pain cost" minus "Solution cost" equals "ROI" — in minimal icon math
- ROI box in #39d353 green for positive framing
- "Response 2 of 5" pill

## Slide 4
**Headline:** Response 3 — The Disaggregation
**Body copy:**
**Say this:** "Let me break down what that actually means per rep per month."

**Why it works:** Large numbers feel heavy. Broken down to daily or per-seat cost, the same number often feels trivial. $24,000/year becomes $65/day. $65/day becomes less than a lunch. Disaggregation turns sticker shock into a non-issue.

**When to use:** When your deal size is mid-market or above and the prospect is reacting to the total number.

**Visual direction:**

- Visual: "$24,000/year → $2,000/month → $65/day" as a cascading breakdown — each line smaller, each in a lighter shade of #814ac8 to #df7afe

## Slide 5
**Headline:** Response 4 — The Competitor Comparison
**Body copy:**
**Say this:** "How does that compare to what you're spending on [current solution or manual process]?"

**Why it works:** Makes the prospect compare you to their actual status quo cost, not an imagined ideal. Often reveals that they're already spending more — just in scattered, invisible ways. This isn't aggressive. It's clarifying.

**When to use:** When you know or suspect they're using a competing product or a manual process with hidden costs.

**Visual direction:**
- Background: #111111
- Two-column card: "Your solution" (left, #814ac8) vs "Their current status quo" (right, #444444) — cost placeholder boxes in each column
- Script line above both columns in the dark card
- Callout: "Hidden costs don't feel like costs until someone names them." — #df7afe italic
- "Response 4 of 5" pill

## Slide 6
**Headline:** Response 5 — The Silence
**Body copy:**
**Say this:** Nothing.

**Why it works:** After they say "it's too expensive," most reps panic and immediately justify, discount, or collapse. Don't. Count to four in your head. Let the silence breathe. 7 times out of 10, the prospect fills it themselves — often with a softer version of the objection or the real concern underneath.

**When to use:** Always. Use silence before any other response. It's the most underused tool in sales.

**Visual direction:**
- Background: #000000
- Centre of slide: just the word "Nothing." in Figtree ExtraBold at 80px in gradient (#814ac8 → #df7afe)
- Below: "Count to 4. Let them fill it." in #c4b8e0 at 18px
- Nothing else on the slide — intentionally minimal
- "Response 5 of 5" pill

## Slide 7
**Headline:** The order matters.
**Body copy:** Try curiosity first. If they answer with a real concern, move to ROI math. If it's still about the number, disaggregate. If they mention a competitor or existing tool, compare. And through all of it — pause before you speak. Every time.
**Visual direction:**
- Background: #0a0a0a
- Flow diagram: 5 responses in a decision-tree style — "Reflexive?" → Response 1 → "Quantifiable pain?" → Response 2 → etc.
- Flow paths in #814ac8 lines, decision boxes in #111111 with #814ac8 border
- Clean, minimal layout — no clutter
